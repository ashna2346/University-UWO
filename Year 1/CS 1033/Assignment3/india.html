INDIA

The country's diversity of culture, people, and tribes are reflected in the wide variety of sporting disciplines in the country. While the mainstream sports played include field hockey, kabaddi, cricket, badminton, shooting, boxing and football, Popular indigenous sports include chess, kho-kho, kite-fighting, leg cricket, polo, snooker, and gillidanda.

Fun Fact

Although, Field Hockey is the National and the most successful sport for India, Cricket is considered as the most popular sport.
     

India is one of the most successful countries when it comes to men’s field hockey and has won 8 gold, one silver, and two bronze medals, more than any other team in the world.

The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is the governing body of Indian cricket and conducts domestic competitions that include the Ranji Trophy, the Duleep Trophy, the Vijay Hazare Trophy, the Deodhar Trophy and the Irani Trophy. The Indian Premier League, a Twenty20 tournament where various city franchises compete and attracts cricketers from all over the world, is one of the biggest sporting leagues and the biggest cricketing league in the world.India has won all of the ICC Tournaments, Including ODI’s World Cup 2 times (1983 & 2011), T20 World Cup 2007, ICC Champions Trophy 2013. So without any doubt, cricket is the most popular sport in India. Indians criekts like Virat Kohli, Sachin Tendulkar, MS Dhoni are some of the renowned players.

Badminton is the second most played sport in India after Cricket. Indian “shuttlers” Saina Nehwal, Pullela Gopichand and P. V. Sindhu are some of the renowned players to have won Olympic medals. Badminton Association of India (BAI) is the governing body for Badminton in India.

Shooting is a competitive and recreational sporting activity. Administered by the The National Rifle Association of India(NRAI), it is an important Olympic sport in India. Some of the popular sharp shooters of india include Abhinav Bindra, Manu Bhaker, Jitu Rai, Heena Sidhu and Saurabh Chaudhary. But largely this sport is not affordable to command Indians because of a lack of shooting ranges and facilities in the country.
