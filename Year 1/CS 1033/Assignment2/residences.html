Residence
     Residence life is more than just a place to rest your head, it is a life changing experience in which you will make life long friends!

Fun Fact:

Originally all residences were named after local rivers.

If you are driving around Southwestern Ontario, you might see rivers with the same name as Western residences.

There are three main styles of residences at Western.

Below are residences styles and the building within each category:

Traditional Style - one or two students in a small room and a shared washroom for the floor.  

  • Delaware Hall
  • Medway Sydenham
  • Saugeen-Maitland Hall
  
Back to top     

Suite Style - one student per bedroom with a shared kitchen between 4 students. 

  • Alumni House
  • Elgin Hall
  • Essex Hall
  • Lambton Hall
  • London Hall
  
Back to top     

Hybrid Style - two students per bedroom but still a bit more private than the traditional style as the bathroom is just shared between 2 students.

  • Ontario Hall
  • Perth Hall

Traditional Style

Opened in 1965, Delaware literally marks an X on the campus!

Medway Hall was constructed in 1958 and then Sydenham was added in 1961.

Opened in 1969, Saugeen was the first co-ed residence on campus.
